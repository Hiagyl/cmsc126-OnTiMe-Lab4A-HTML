Great Pyramid of Giza

The Great Pyramid of Giza is the oldest and largest of the three pyramids in the Giza pyramid complex. It is the only one of the Seven Wonders still in existence.

Colossus of Rhodes

The Colossus of Rhodes was a statue of the Greek sun god Helios, erected in the city of Rhodes. It was destroyed in an earthquake in 226 BCE.

Lighthouse of Alexandria

The Lighthouse of Alexandria was a towering structure built to guide sailors into the harbor. It was one of the tallest man-made structures for many centuries.

Mausoleum at Halicarnassus

The Mausoleum at Halicarnassus was a tomb built for Mausolus, a satrap of the Persian Empire. It was known for its elaborate sculptures and architecture.

Temple of Artemis

The Temple of Artemis was a Greek temple dedicated to the goddess Artemis. It was destroyed and rebuilt multiple times.

Statue of Zeus at Olympia

The Statue of Zeus at Olympia was a giant seated figure of the Greek god Zeus. It was one of the most famous statues of the ancient world.

Hanging Gardens of Babylon

The Hanging Gardens of Babylon were described as a remarkable feat of engineering with lush greenery and waterfalls. Their existence remains debated.
